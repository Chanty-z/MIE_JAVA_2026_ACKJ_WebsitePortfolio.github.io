My first essay i've worked on at Mercer Inter ED

Valorant is a game that is very dear to me, it is incredibly competitive and requires a lot of creativity when it comes to strategizing your game plan. I've spent hundreds and hundreds of hours studying this sport and used that knowledge to create my own strategies for an esports team i was on. Here are some of my favorite strategies ive spent hours and hours creating:

"haven defense plan we take a lobby early with rechargeable util and a crazy ruan neon. cypher anchors garage with trip and one way cage. omen jump peeks c long, blinds and smokes on site to stall push". This is the exact wording i used to describe my strategy to my team, ofcourse i went further into detail in person but i digress. To take A lobby we use a Breach to slow the attackers down allowing the Neon to rush in and take space. Further ensuring the Neon wins the engagement a sova dart is used to track the exact location of an enemy that might not have been slowed by the breach stun. Using the rechargeable utility first allows for sustained pressure throughout the round. The Cypher player set up in garage is purely to gather information for early rotations and ensuring we dont get flanked. Cypher also has a one way cage on top of garage door which allows for easy stalling and a potential kill. The Omen anchors C alone because they can stop a rush the best with their blind and can still provide map presence even when far away from team. Even if the Omen gets rushed, they can simply blind to stall, get a kill or tp off site to help retake. This strategy focuses on taking control of A lobby early on and using that control to set up a strong defense on the A site, allowing for easy retakes on B and C site. The reason we dont take C site is because A lobby allows for easy access to both A long and A sewers where as C long only gives access to well C long, it is by far the most powerful area of the map for attackers, allowing for annoying lurking oppurtunities.
